Why this happens
Your neurons stop responding to things that don't change. It's called Neural Adaptation. The word was perfectly clear the whole time. Your brain just… stopped seeing it properly.
Now think about a 60-minute lecture. You're staring at a screen that barely changes. Your mind does the exact same thing — it starts to blur what you're watching.
That's how focus drops. That's why you can't retain what's being taught. And that is what separates one student from another.
